# Spleen — Anatomy Overview

The spleen is a single, unpaired organ located in the upper left portion of
the abdomen, tucked beneath the left side of the rib cage, posterior and
lateral to the stomach and superior to the left kidney. It is the smallest of
the four organs typically segmented in an abdominal MRI study covering the
liver, kidneys, and spleen, and normally shows a correspondingly smaller
per-slice coverage percentage than the liver.

Functionally, the spleen filters blood, recycles old red blood cells, stores
platelets, and participates in immune responses. It has a relatively
homogeneous internal texture on imaging, similar in some respects to liver
parenchyma but generally smaller and more variable in exact size and shape
from person to person and with respiration phase. Because it is smaller and
sits near the edge of a typical axial slice, it is more prone to partial
volume effects — where a slice cuts through only part of the organ — which
can affect both manual and automated measurements more than for larger organs
like the liver.
