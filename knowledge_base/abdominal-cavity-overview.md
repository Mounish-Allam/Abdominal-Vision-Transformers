# Abdominal Cavity — Relative Organ Layout

On a single axial (cross-sectional) abdominal MRI slice, the four organs
segmented in this project occupy characteristic, roughly consistent relative
positions, though the exact slice level determines which organs are visible
and how much of each appears:

- The **liver** occupies the upper right and central abdomen, and is the
  largest organ by cross-sectional area on most slices where it appears.
- The **right kidney** sits in the right retroperitoneum, typically slightly
  lower than the left kidney because the liver displaces it downward.
- The **left kidney** sits in the left retroperitoneum, at a similar or
  slightly higher level than the right kidney.
- The **spleen** sits in the upper left abdomen, roughly at a similar
  craniocaudal level to the upper liver and left kidney, but with a smaller
  cross-sectional footprint.

Because these organs sit at different craniocaudal levels, not every organ
appears on every slice — a slice near the top of the liver may not yet show
the kidneys, and a slice at the level of the mid-kidneys may show a much
smaller sliver of liver or none of the spleen. This is a normal consequence of
3D anatomy being sampled by a single 2D slice, not a segmentation error.
